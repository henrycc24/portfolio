## Overview

This project developed a proof-of-concept virtual fencing system for livestock to make grazing management more flexible, lower-cost, and easier to monitor.

## Motivation / Problem

Traditional fencing is expensive to install, labor-intensive to maintain, and difficult to move when grazing boundaries change. This is especially important for solar grazing, where livestock manage vegetation around solar arrays while avoiding sensitive equipment and exclusion zones.

## My Role

I contributed to mechanical and electrical integration, subsystem integration, dashboard support, and prototype testing as part of a four-person team.

## System Architecture

The IoT solution includes a livestock collar and a browser-based monitoring dashboard. The collar integrates GPS location tracking, motion sensing, LoRa communication, audio boundary feedback, and solar-assisted battery power.

## Hardware / Electrical Design

The final prototype included a downsized enclosure, a second solar panel, and an improved harness approach. The dashboard shows live animal position, digital fence boundaries, battery status, speed, and last update time.

## Results

The project integrated mechanical, electrical, communication, and software subsystems into a functional semester-scale prototype developed with feedback from Jess Gray of Gray's LAMBscaping.

## Future Work

Improve waterproofing, harness reliability, GPS correction accuracy, power optimization, communication range, and outdoor durability testing.
