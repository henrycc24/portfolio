## Overview

Oaken Optics is an interactive piece of wall art built from an array of actuated wooden tiles that respond to wireless controller input and live camera input.

## Design Goal

The project aims to remind audiences of the importance of art in engineering — especially in an age of generative AI where creativity must remain human-driven.

## Design Motivation

Modern engineering would not exist without art, because innovation cannot exist without creativity. We wanted the final artifact to feel dynamic and fluid while still being built from hardware and software.

## Fabrication Process

We started with low-fidelity cardboard prototypes to validate individual cell mechanics before scaling toward a modular tile array. Early work focused on perfecting one reliable cell design that could be replicated across the grid.

## Materials & Iterations

Initial prototypes used cardboard, string, and duct tape. Later iterations moved toward laser-cut and fabricated wooden tile modules with synchronized actuation.

## My Role

I contributed to hardware design, prototyping, motion control integration, and fabrication of the actuated tile system.

## Reflection

The project reinforced that rapid prototyping is most effective when each iteration tests one mechanical or control assumption at a time.

## Future Work

Scale to a full 8×8 tile array, improve multi-actuator synchronization, and refine wireless and camera-driven motion commands.
